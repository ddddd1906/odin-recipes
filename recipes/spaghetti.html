Description

Spaghetti carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper.

The dish is known for its creamy texture and rich flavor, making it a favorite among pasta lovers.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Pancetta or guanciale
  • Eggs
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Black pepper
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve some of the pasta water before draining.
  2. In a pan, cook the pancetta or guanciale until crispy. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated Parmesan cheese until well combined.
  4. Return the pan with pancetta to low heat. Add the cooked spaghetti and toss to combine.
  5. Remove the pan from heat and quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta, tossing vigorously to create a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water to loosen it up.
  6. Season with black pepper and salt to taste. Serve immediately with extra Parmesan cheese on top.
